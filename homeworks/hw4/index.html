Assignement 1 Explain the meaning of "converge" used in "the frequency converges to the probability...".

When Frequency Meets Probability

In statistics and probability, when we say that "the frequency converges to the probability," we are referring to a specific concept of convergence formalized by a fundamental theorem: the Law of Large Numbers. It is not a different type of convergence from mathematical convergence, but rather a specific application of mathematical convergence to the world of random variables.

  • Simple Definition: This theorem states that if you repeat a random experiment (like flipping a coin) a very large number of times, the relative frequency of an event (e.g., the percentage of times it came up "heads") will get closer and closer to its theoretical probability (50%, or 0.5, for a fair coin).
  • Convergence: The term "converge" means exactly this: as the number of trials (n) increases, the difference between the observed relative frequency (f_n) and the theoretical probability (p) becomes smaller and smaller, tending to zero.

This is the important distinction. It is not the same convergence as a simple numerical sequence (like 1/n converging to 0), but a stochastic convergence (or "in probability").
